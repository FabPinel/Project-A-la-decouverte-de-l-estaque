Chichi-frégis of course !

Chichi-frégis are the most famous specialty in all of Marseille. Everyone in Marseille knows what a fuss is. Although you can find frills in the city center of Marseille, it is in the 3 chichis shacks of L'Estaque that you should taste this little non-dietary pleasure that is often confused with Churros. The big difference with the churros is that the Churros is twisted, thin and short: the Chichi is thick, puffy and it measures about 8 inches. It can be eaten plain, barely crunchy on the top and very airy inside, rolled in sugar, garnished with hazelnut paste, or even covered with whipped cream. It is part of what we now call Street Food but it was invented in the 1930s and it was the workers in the tile factories and factories of Estaque who popularized it. They ate it standing, under a tree, or in the harbor. A real pleasure break! The Chichi has become over the years, a goal of stroll: for many Marseilles, the Sunday exit passes by the Estaque where one stops back from swimming on the Blue Coast (Carry, Sausset etc ... ). Almost a must for those who will tell their friends about their perfect day!

Without forgetting the "panisses" !

Panisses are a must with chichi-frégis. Made from chickpea flour, à l'Estaque, it is sold in the form of slices 2 cm thick, already cut and fried. Often by the dozen, sprinkled with fleur de sel and pepper. They can be enjoyed standing or on the terraces of the Estaque cafes, in a paper cone. In general, the Marseillais always take panisses with their fuss when coming into the neighborhood. The “chichi / panisses” combination is “The” specialty to try in this district of Marseille.

Our Restaurant's Choice!

Among the restaurants in Estaque, l'Etincelle has a special place. Nestled in the heart of L'Estaque facing the port and the chichis huts of Marseilles, its panoramic terrace overlooking the sea attracts and makes you dream. The dishes offered, made from fresh and regional products such as fish, shellfish and meats, bear the “HOME MADE” label. Your taste buds, exhilarated by these tastes, will be able to taste even more with the many choices of wines kept by their large cellar with wine for aging. In addition, no one is left behind since the restaurant is equipped to accommodate people with reduced mobility. Do not hesitate to go and discover this typical Estaque restaurant from Monday to Sunday at 82-84 Plaque de l'Estaque in Marseille.

Don't forget the dessert!

The Gelati Amore Glacier is a Marseilles glacier currently with four establishments in the city, including one in Estaque. In a square in the shade of the plane trees, colorful and lively chairs catch the eye. In addition to this cheerful decor, the Gelati Amore offers artisanal ice creams just as colorful with around fifty flavors, ranging from pink to brown, from strawberry to chocolate. But to suit all tastes, it also offers sweet or savory homemade pancakes, waffles, frozen yogurts or granita, as well as a wide range of hot, cold or frozen drinks. Come and enjoy a moment of relaxation full of freshness at 86 Estaque Beach, but beware: only from March to September.
